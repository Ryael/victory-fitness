My Story

Many years ago, I was running a high-stress kitchen, working 16-hour days as a chef, and barely seeing my kids.

I'd come home exhausted. My body was running on fumes—every ache, every skipped meal, every late-night binge catching up to me.

If you find yourself nodding or muttering “yes” to any of these questions, there's no time better than the present to your fitness into your own hands.

I wasn't just overeating I was using food to cope. When the stress of work became overwhelming or when the guilt of missing family moments weighed heavily on me, I'd turn to late-night snacks and junk food for comfort. It was a vicious cycle: eating to feel better temporarily, only to wake up the next day feeling worse—physically and emotionally.

Underneath it all, I was battling feelings of isolation and low self-esteem. I'd convinced myself I had to carry everything on my own shoulders, and that asking for help or showing vulnerability would make me less of a man. But instead of building strength, I was slowly breaking myself down.
